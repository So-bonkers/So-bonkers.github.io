Why it exists

One tool. Three promises.

🧠

AI-Native from the ground up

Not AI bolted on as an afterthought. Hum-to-MIDI, stem separation, adaptive quantisation, pattern generation — the whole workflow is AI-accelerated, running entirely on your hardware.

// no API keys. no internet. no latency.

🔒

Your sessions, forever yours

100% offline. Open .echoforge format. No subscription that can expire, no cloud that can go down, no company that can be acquired and shut your project files out.

// read every session file in a text editor.

∞

Free. Forever. No trap.

No freemium tier that locks stems export. No annual subscription. No feature flags for paying users. The full tool, for every producer on earth, regardless of where they live or what they can afford.

// $0 now. $0 always.

🥁

AI Stem Separation local

Drop any track — commercial release, sample, or reference mix — and pull out drums, bass, melody, and vocals as individual stems. Everything runs on your CPU/GPU. Nothing leaves your machine.

🎹

Hum to MIDI

Sing, hum, or whistle a melody into your mic. EchoForge converts it to quantised MIDI in real time. No instrument, no keyboard, no theory needed — just the idea in your head.

🎛️

MIDI Editor + AI Quantisation

Full piano roll with AI-assisted groove quantisation that preserves your feel while fixing the timing. Pattern variation engine generates fills and variations from your existing clips.

💾

Open .echoforge Format

Your sessions are plain-text readable. Every plugin state, every automation curve, every sample reference — documented and open. Version control your music in git if you want to.

⚡

Hardware-Aware Inference

Automatically benchmarks your machine on first run and selects the right model tier. Runs lean on a 2018 laptop. Runs fast on a GPU workstation. No manual config.

🔀

Pattern Generation

Highlight a MIDI region and ask for variations — tighter groove, sparser arrangement, genre-shifted. The model runs locally and doesn't touch your stem audio.

🎙️

Real-Time Pitch Detection

Sub-100ms pitch tracking from any audio input. Sing into the arrangement view and watch notes appear as you go. Works polyphonically on simple chord voicings.

🧩

Context-Aware Suggestions

EchoForge reads your current key, scale, and rhythmic grid and suggests MIDI continuations that fit. Toggle off in settings if you just want to be left alone.

🗂️

Arrangement View

Timeline-based arrangement editor with clip launching, scene switching, and per-clip automation. Familiar to anyone who's used a DAW before.

🔌

VST3 / AU Plugin Support

Load your existing instrument and effect plugins. EchoForge doesn't lock you into a proprietary sound engine. Keep the plugins you've already bought.

🌊

Mixer with Routing

Full channel strip: gain, EQ, dynamics, send/return routing. Nothing exotic — just a clean, fast mixer that gets out of your way.

⌨️

Keyboard-First Design

Every action has a keyboard shortcut. The mouse is optional. Designed to keep experienced producers in flow without reaching for a menu.

Learning Mode — an optional overlay for producers who want to understand what they're doing. Toggle it on when you're curious, off when you're in flow.

📖

Scale & Chord Naming

Detects your key, names every chord you play, and shows the scale degree in real time. The information panel floats over the piano roll — dismiss it with Escape.

🌍

10,000+ Cultural Examples

"You're using a Dorian mode — Miles Davis used it in So What, and it runs through Ethiopian tizita music." Connects your choices to 80+ musical traditions.

🎚️

Genre Lens

Select a genre — trap, lo-fi, afrobeats, jazz, classical — and see an overlay explaining what producers in that tradition actually do differently in their arrangements.

💡

Chord Suggestions + Reasoning

Chord suggestions shown with the theory reasoning visible, not hidden. "Try Fm → C → Dm — a ii–VII–i that deepens the mood." Accept, reject, or ignore.

Learning Mode is off by default. It doesn't change how the DAW works — it just adds a context layer when you want it.
